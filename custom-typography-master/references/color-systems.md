# Color Systems Reference

Named palette archetypes by era, aesthetic, and use context.
Specific color language produces specific results in Nano Banana Pro.
Never say "vintage colors" ‚Äî say which vintage colors.

---

## Two-Color Systems (Most Powerful for Badge Work)

Two colors is the most historically authentic palette for badge and merch work.
Every major constraint forces better decisions.

### Heritage Americana Palettes
- `warm cream + deep navy blue` ‚Äî railroad, grain company, WPA
- `aged parchment + brick red` ‚Äî seed packet, hardware store
- `off-white + forest green` ‚Äî park service, outdoor heritage
- `natural tan + dark brown` ‚Äî leather goods, outfitter
- `cream + black` ‚Äî the universal workhorse ‚Äî use when in doubt

### Athletic Two-Color
- `cardinal red + cream` ‚Äî eastern seaboard classic
- `royal blue + gold` ‚Äî mid-American collegiate
- `forest green + white` ‚Äî New England prep
- `maroon + silver` ‚Äî modern premium athletic
- `black + white` ‚Äî always competitive

### Dark/Gothic Two-Color
- `black + blood red` ‚Äî aggressive, metal, fight sports
- `black + pale bone white` ‚Äî dark heritage
- `charcoal + amber` ‚Äî whiskey, dark americana

## Foil and Metallic Language

Precise metallic language for Nano Banana Pro.

### Gold
- `warm 18k gold foil, slight warmth in the yellow tone` ‚Äî premium heritage
- `antique gold, slightly muted metallic` ‚Äî aged premium
- `bright 24k gold, high metallic sheen` ‚Äî championship, award
- `brushed gold, matte metallic with directional texture` ‚Äî modern luxury

### Silver
- `bright silver foil, cool metallic` ‚Äî modern premium
- `brushed silver, matte directional texture` ‚Äî contemporary
- `aged silver, slightly oxidized surface` ‚Äî heritage metallic
- `chrome, high-gloss reflective` ‚Äî action sports, moto

### Specialty
- `rose gold, warm pink metallic` ‚Äî beauty, lifestyle
- `copper foil, warm red metallic` ‚Äî craft, artisan, warmth
- `bronze, dark warm metallic` ‚Äî heritage, aged award

## Ground/Background Colors

The field color is as important as the type color.

### Paper/Stock Grounds
- `warm off-white, aged paper quality` ‚Äî heritage, letterpress
- `kraft brown paper ground` ‚Äî artisan, raw
- `natural linen texture ground` ‚Äî premium, textile
- `cream ground with subtle paper grain` ‚Äî neutral premium
- `black ground` ‚Äî premium, moto, streetwear
- `deep navy ground` ‚Äî premium, collegiate

### Garment-Context Grounds
When the badge is for apparel:
- `on a black garment ground` ‚Äî neutral, universal
- `on charcoal heather fabric texture` ‚Äî contemporary
- `on cream/natural garment` ‚Äî heritage, premium
- `on cardinal red garment ground` ‚Äî athletic

## Color Description Precision

**Wrong:**
`vintage colors`

**Right:**
`two-color limited palette: warm cream ground (#F5F0E8 range) with deep indigo-navy
blue type (#1B2A4A range), slight ink density variation as if letterpress`

**Wrong:**
`gold and black`

**Right:**
`warm 18k gold foil treatment on letterforms over matte black field,
the gold has slight warmth and a premium metallic sheen`

**Wrong:**
`faded colors`

**Right:**
`sun-bleached palette ‚Äî saturations at approximately 60% of full value,
all tones shifted slightly warm, colors feel like they've been outside for a decade`

## Nano Banana Pro Color Notes

- The model responds to specific color mood descriptions, not just swatches
- Describing the light quality (warm, cool, muted, saturated) activates the right palette
- Naming the source material (letterpress on kraft, screen-print on aged cotton)
  triggers contextually correct color behavior
- Two-color is more reliable than full color for badge work
- When specifying foil ‚Äî always add a surface quality descriptor (matte, glossy, brushed)
