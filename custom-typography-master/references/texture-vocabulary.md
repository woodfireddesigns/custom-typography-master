# Texture Vocabulary Reference

Texture is what separates a Nano Banana output that looks handcrafted from one that
looks generated. One precise texture descriptor changes everything.

---

## Print Process Textures

### Letterpress
The gold standard for heritage and authentic feel.
- `letterpress ink trap` ‚Äî ink pooling slightly at enclosed corners and angles
- `letterpress impression` ‚Äî slight deboss on the physical surface
- `letterpress ink spread` ‚Äî stroke edges slightly soft where ink kissed the stock
- `uneven ink distribution, letterpress quality` ‚Äî organic ink density variation
- `letterpress with paper grain showing through light ink coverage areas`

**Best used for:** Heritage Americana, Craft Beverage, Dark Americana, Food/Artisan

### Screen Print
The apparel and poster standard.
- `screen-print on garment stock` ‚Äî slightly rough, fabric grain visible
- `screen-print halftone grain` ‚Äî dots visible at edges of solid areas
- `screen-print ink bleed` ‚Äî slight expansion at sharp corners
- `off-registration screen-print artifact` ‚Äî secondary color slightly misaligned
- `screen-print with ink crawl` ‚Äî ink slightly pulled along fabric surface grain
- `faded screen-print, washed multiple times` ‚Äî color degradation, cracking

**Best used for:** Athletic/Collegiate, Action Sports, Concert Posters, Streetwear

### Risograph / Offset Print
Two-color or limited palette printing artifacts.
- `risograph two-color, slight misregistration between layers`
- `risograph ink texture, slight blotchy variation`
- `offset lithograph with halftone visible` ‚Äî dots in mid-tone areas
- `offset print on newsprint, slight show-through from reverse side`
- `color photostat texture` ‚Äî 1970s-80s print process feel

**Best used for:** Concert Posters, Music Venue, Craft Beverage, Surf

### Wood Type / Letterpress Poster
The Hatch Show Print reference.
- `wood type letterform with slight ink squeeze at stroke edges`
- `heavy letterpress impression from wood type, ink density variation`
- `wood type ornament rules with uneven printing across length`
- `woodcut illustration texture alongside letterpress type`

**Best used for:** Concert Posters, Heritage Americana, Dark Americana

## Surface and Material Textures

### Paper and Stock
- `aged off-white paper stock, visible grain` ‚Äî neutral premium
- `kraft paper stock, brown surface grain visible` ‚Äî artisan, raw
- `newsprint stock, low-contrast surface` ‚Äî DIY, zine, independent
- `French paper texture, premium tactile quality` ‚Äî screen-print poster standard
- `dampened paper, slight wrinkle texture` ‚Äî aged, archive feel
- `torn paper edge on badge perimeter` ‚Äî rough, handmade, craft

### Fabric
- `canvas duck cloth surface, warp and weft visible`
- `heavyweight 100% cotton garment texture` ‚Äî apparel graphic ground
- `faded athletic fabric, slightly pilled surface`
- `felt pennant fabric, napped surface under screen-print`
- `denim texture ground`

### Leather and Hard Goods
- `tooled leather surface, embossed depth in letterforms`
- `debossed into soft leather, slight compression visible`
- `rubber stamp impression on leather surface`
- `worn leather wallet texture, crease lines visible`

### Metal and Enamel
- `enamel pin surface, cloisonn√© division between color fields`
- `die-cast metal badge, slight surface texture, oxidized edges`
- `engraved metal plate, fine chisel marks visible`
- `silver plate surface with light reflection gradient`
- `stamped metal tag, slight warp on corners`

### Wood
- `woodcut texture, grain running through solid areas`
- `burned into wood, char texture on letterform fills`
- `wood plank surface under hand-painted lettering`
- `laser-etched wood, sharp contrast, fine burn detail`

## Aging and Distress Treatments

### Light Distress (Premium, Intentional)
- `subtle halftone grain overlay on all elements`
- `slight ink trap at stroke intersections, otherwise clean`
- `paper grain showing through light ink areas only`
- `edges of solid fills slightly softened, print quality`

### Medium Distress (Vintage, Authentic)
- `worn edges on badge perimeter, 30% breakdown`
- `scattered light erosion on solid fills, salt-and-pepper texture`
- `partially faded solid areas, as if sun-bleached`
- `slight cracking in the ink surface on fill areas`
- `deliberate worn corners and edge detail`

### Heavy Distress (Rugged, Extreme)
- `heavy weathering, 50% ink breakdown on all solid areas`
- `type legible but heavily worn, survival of letterforms only`
- `faded to near-ghost, vestige of original print visible`
- Use sparingly ‚Äî heavy distress can fight readability

## Dimensional Treatments

### Emboss / Deboss
- `blind emboss, no ink, pure surface relief`
- `color emboss, ink with raised surface`
- `letterforms debossed into surface, shadow visible in relief`

### Foil / Metallic
- `gold foil treatment on letterforms, warm metallic sheen`
- `silver foil type, cool metallic surface`
- `spot UV on letterforms over matte surface, gloss contrast`
- `holographic foil, rainbow spectrum shift on letterforms`

## Applying Textures in Prompts

**The wrong way:**
`with a vintage texture`

**The right way:**
`letterpress ink trap texture on all letterforms, uneven ink distribution
as if hand-set type, paper grain visible through light solid areas,
off-white aged paper stock ground`

**Multiple textures** (when appropriate):
Lead with the primary treatment, add secondary as modifier:
`screen-print on heavy canvas duck cloth, halftone grain visible at
color edges, slight off-registration between the two color layers,
50-year faded feel on the fill areas`
