# Style Systems Reference

20 named style profiles. Each includes: aesthetic DNA, letterform character,
texture defaults, color archetypes, key references, and prompt activation vocabulary.

---

## 1. HERITAGE AMERICANA

**Aesthetic DNA**
Early 20th century American commercial art. Seed packets, cattle brands, railroad
typography, hardware store signage, union labels, WPA-era poster work.
The feeling of something built to last.

**Letterform Character**
- Heavy slab serifs with bracketed thick-to-thin transitions
- Condensed grotesque all-caps for secondary lines
- Occasional inline detail on main display letterforms
- Tight, deliberate letterspacing — not wide-open tracking
- Common pairings: bold expanded slab + tight condensed sans

**Texture Defaults**
Letterpress ink trap, aged paper grain, slight ink bleed at stroke intersections,
deliberate worn edges on filled areas

**Color Archetypes**
- Cream/warm white + deep navy + brick red
- Tan/aged parchment + black + forest green
- Off-white + burgundy + gold
Two or three colors maximum — more feels wrong for this style.

**Key References**
Carhartt heritage labels, Filson catalog typography, old Pendleton woolens tags,
Levi's Red Tab era, vintage American seed company labels, WPA national park posters,
Jack Daniel's original label typography

**Prompt Activation Vocabulary**
`vintage American heritage label` / `1920s-1930s American commercial lettering` /
`WPA-era sign painting aesthetic` / `heritage workwear brand badge` /
`aged letterpress print with ink trap texture` / `early American trade typography`

## 2. VINTAGE ATHLETIC / COLLEGIATE

**Aesthetic DNA**
1950s-1970s American sports identity. Championship pennants, varsity jackets,
gym uniforms, team programs, stadium signage. Feels earned, not designed.

**Letterform Character**
- Ultra-condensed block letters with heavy stroke weight
- Strong vertical stress, minimal stroke contrast
- Letterspacing often set very tight or even slightly overlapping
- Number forms treated as first-class display elements
- Script alternates for team names — thick-thin contrast, slight backward lean

**Texture Defaults**
Faded athletic fabric feel, worn chenille patch texture, screen-print ink breakdown,
slightly distressed edges as if washed 50 times

**Color Archetypes**
- Cardinal red + athletic gold + cream
- Royal blue + maize + white
- Forest green + orange + cream
- Maroon + silver + black

**Key References**
1960s-70s university athletic programs, classic NBA hardwood uniforms,
vintage NFL Championship programs, original Champion sportswear tags,
Rawlings baseball uniform lettering, classic high school varsity jacket patches

**Prompt Activation Vocabulary**
`vintage collegiate athletic lettering` / `1960s varsity sports typography` /
`classic American athletic emblem` / `worn chenille patch aesthetic` /
`championship pennant typography` / `varsity jacket felt letter style`

## 3. PACIFIC NORTHWEST OUTDOOR / ADVENTURE

**Aesthetic DNA**
Premium outdoor brand identity. Yeti, Howler Brothers, Patagonia's more rugged
sub-lines, Fjallraven. Feels like it belongs on a dry bag in the Cascades.
Typography is confident and clean but has texture and weight.

**Letterform Character**
- Wide, grounded grotesque or humanist sans-serif all-caps
- Medium-heavy weight, deliberate stroke width
- Geometric undertones with slight warmth
- Secondary lines often in tight condensed sans
- Occasional slab serif for heritage-leaning versions

**Texture Defaults**
Screen-print on weathered canvas, rubber stamp on kraft paper,
deboss impression on leather goods, subtle halftone grain

**Color Archetypes**
- Deep teal + off-white + natural tan
- Slate blue + burnt orange + cream
- Olive drab + cream + charcoal
- Forest + bone + rust

**Key References**
Howler Brothers badge system, Yeti lid badges, REI vintage identity,
Filson Modern (not heritage), Patagonia black label marks,
vintage National Park concessionaire patches

**Prompt Activation Vocabulary**
`premium outdoor adventure badge` / `Pacific Northwest brand identity aesthetic` /
`rugged outdoor gear typography` / `clean but weathered outdoor apparel graphic` /
`modern heritage outdoor badge on canvas texture`

## 4. ACTION SPORTS / SKATE / SNOW

**Aesthetic DNA**
The world Lincoln Design Co, DC Shoes, and Alien Workshop inhabit.
Aggressive, irreverent, technically impressive. Counter-culture with
craft underneath. Not punk — that's separate — but definitely not corporate.

**Letterform Character**
- Distorted or custom-warped letterforms
- Heavy weight with deliberate imperfection
- Stencil elements, negative space cutouts
- Oblique stress, pushed angles
- Mixed case is acceptable; all-caps with intentional breaks
- Graffiti influence without being literal graffiti

**Texture Defaults**
Spray paint overspray edges, sticker weathering, scratched foil,
concrete surface under type, worn grip tape texture reference

**Color Archetypes**
- Black + neon (acid yellow, electric blue, hot pink)
- Black + white + single accent
- Stark contrast, high impact
- Occasional 90s-influenced tri-color: teal, purple, magenta

**Key References**
Lincoln Design Co (primary reference), Alien Workshop early era,
DC Shoes mid-2000s, Zero Skateboards, Powell-Peralta, Thrasher magazine
masthead treatment, Independent Trucks logo language

**Prompt Activation Vocabulary**
`aggressive action sports badge graphic` / `skate culture typography, post-2000` /
`Lincoln Design Co aesthetic, action sports identity` / `bold distorted letterforms,
street culture influence` / `high-contrast impact graphic for skateboard culture`

## 5. CRAFT BEVERAGE / BREWERY

**Aesthetic DNA**
What craft beer labels perfected: the collision of heritage Americana with
modern graphic sensibility. Clean execution, typographic layering,
illustration-integrated type. Dogfish Head, Founders, Stone, Allagash label DNA.

**Letterform Character**
- Layered typographic hierarchy — 3-4 distinct type weights/styles in one composition
- Headline in strong display slab or condensed gothic
- Secondary in refined script or small-caps serif
- Descriptor lines in tight condensed sans
- Deliberate, studied mix — feels curated not chaotic

**Texture Defaults**
Kraft label stock texture, offset print halftone, slight wet label feel,
aged paper with visible grain, letterpress impression on soft label stock

**Color Archetypes**
- Kraft brown + black + single accent (copper, green, cream)
- Deep navy + cream + amber gold
- Forest green + cream + burnt orange
- Black + gold + white (premium tier)

**Key References**
Founders Brewing label system, Dogfish Head mid-era labels,
Allagash white label, Stone Brewing gothic labels, Tröegs Brewing,
Three Floyds Dark Lord label treatments, Lost Abbey typography

**Prompt Activation Vocabulary**
`craft brewery label typography` / `artisanal beverage label composition` /
`layered typographic hierarchy on kraft paper stock` /
`small-batch craft label aesthetic, letterpress quality` /
`beer label badge with woodcut texture and slab serif headline`

**Letterform Character**
- Gothic blackletter influence but westernized and legible
- Heavy weight, tight spacing, vertical stress
- Diamond and shield shapes as containing forms

Cracked leather surface, enamel pin feel, worn iron-on patch,
heavy ink on rough stock, old decal peel texture

**Color Archetypes**

- Metallic gold or silver as accent

**Key References**
Harley-Davidson vintage shield badges, vintage AMA racing programs,
Biltwell Inc product identity, Roland Sands Design aesthetics,
vintage motorcycle club cut patches, Triumph UK heritage marks

vintage military surplus markings. Not Call of Duty — actual historical
military graphic language. Quiet, precise, earned.

**Letterform Character**
- Stencil cuts through letterforms (deliberate, not accidental)
- Strong gothic or grotesque, heavy and reliable
- Minimal decoration — functional beauty
- Numbering systems integrated with letters
- Unit patch circular compositions

**Texture Defaults**
Stencil overspray, worn canvas, stamped olive drab,
faded unit patch on worn wool, rubber-stamp marking

**Color Archetypes**
- Olive drab + black + cream
- Desert tan + dark brown
- Navy + cream
- Black + safety orange (ordnance aesthetic)

**Key References**
WWII USAAF nose art lettering, vintage military surplus typography,
airborne division patch design, Navy/Army stencil marking systems,
vintage G.I. equipment markings

**Prompt Activation Vocabulary**

## 10. PREMIUM STREETWEAR / LUXURY HYPEBEAST

**Aesthetic DNA**
Supreme's gothic simplicity meets Off-White's industrial intervention.
Palace's absurdist wit. The typographic confidence of brands that don't
need to explain themselves. Clean but loaded.

**Letterform Character**
- Either ultra-minimal (one weight, no decoration) or ultra-expressive (maximum distortion)
- High contrast between these two modes — nothing in the middle
- Gothic blackletter as luxury signal (Supreme model)
- Helvetica ultra-bold or extended sans pushed to extremes
- No drop shadows, no bevels, no gradients

**Texture Defaults**
Screen-print on heavy garment stock (minimal texture), OR stark no-texture flat graphic
with clean closed shapes. Either extreme. Never middle.

**Color Archetypes**
- Black on white / white on black (default, primary)
- Single accent: red, orange, or safety yellow on black
- Full-color only when referencing 90s nostalgia

**Key References**
Supreme box logo typographic language, Off-White industrial text treatment,
Palace skateboarding serif marks, Stussy original script,
Cactus Jack type language, Balenciaga deconstructed type

**Prompt Activation Vocabulary**
`premium streetwear graphic, screen-print aesthetic` /
`Supreme-influenced gothic wordmark, luxury street culture` /
`minimal high-contrast apparel graphic, black on white` /
`Off-White industrial text treatment, heavy sans-serif` /
`hypebeast brand label typography, ultra-clean execution`

## 12. MUSIC VENUE / CONCERT POSTER

**Aesthetic DNA**
The legacy of Hatch Show Print meets Austin-era screen-printing.
Concert posters as high design. The Fillmore, Cactus Club,
Third Man Records' visual world. Typography as spectacle.

**Letterform Character**
- Expressive wood type (heavy, condensed, imperfect)
- High contrast between headline scale and body text scale
- Mixed type styles — deliberate, structured chaos
- Letterforms set at angles, in arcs, in unexpected orientations
- Wood type imperfections: slight printing unevenness, ink squeeze

**Texture Defaults**
Screen-print on French paper, wood type impression with ink spread,
risograph two-color registration artifact, Xerox halftone grain

**Color Archetypes**
- Two-color risograph (pink + teal, black + yellow, red + blue)
- High-contrast single color on colored stock
- Third Man Records: black + yellow is the defining reference

**Key References**
Hatch Show Print Nashville letterpress work, Firehouse Letterpress,
Third Man Records yellow-and-black aesthetic, vintage Fillmore poster typesetting,
Cactus Club Milwaukee show posters, GigPosters.com community work

**Prompt Activation Vocabulary**
`vintage concert poster typography, letterpress aesthetic` /
`Hatch Show Print letterpress wood type composition` /
`screen-printed gig poster, two-color risograph aesthetic` /
`Third Man Records yellow-and-black typographic energy` /
`wood type poster composition, deliberate typographic weight contrast`

## 15. PSYCHEDELIC / RETRO REVIVAL

**Aesthetic DNA**
Wes Wilson and Victor Moscoso's Fillmore West posters. The liquid chrome
of the 1960s. Groovy but structurally rigorous. More than just wavy —
the underlying geometry is strict.

**Letterform Character**
- Letterforms that fill and flow into each other's negative space
- S-curve baseline and cap height variations
- Thick uniform strokes, minimal weight contrast
- Letters expand and contract to fill available space
- Optical illusion plays on positive/negative reversal

**Texture Defaults**
Offset lithograph from 1967, fluorescent color-printed on newsprint,
slight misregistration between colors, softened edges

**Color Archetypes**
- Vibrant complements: magenta/green, purple/gold, red/blue
- Fluorescent accents
- Black outlines separating colors — stained glass logic

**Key References**
Victor Moscoso concert posters, Wes Wilson Fillmore posters,
Big Daddy Roth hot rod culture, Rick Griffin lettering,
1970s AOR rock album typography, Van Halen original logo DNA

**Prompt Activation Vocabulary**
`1960s psychedelic poster lettering, Victor Moscoso style` /
`groovy liquid letterforms, fill-the-negative-space composition` /
`psychedelic rock poster typography, offset lithograph aesthetic` /
`AOR rock album lettering, 1970s psychedelic revival` /
`groovy rounded display type, optically interlocking letterforms`

## 19. TECH / MODERN GEOMETRIC

**Aesthetic DNA**
The type that shows up in SXSW branding, premium SaaS companies, and
the more typographic corners of the Apple design ecosystem. Precision.
Every corner intentional. Swiss grid discipline with American confidence.

**Letterform Character**
- Geometric sans-serif, constructed from circles and straight lines
- Perfect spacing, mathematical consistency
- Ultra-light or ultra-bold — no medium weight
- Negative space as primary design element
- Monospace or fixed-width as deliberate aesthetic choice

**Texture Defaults**
None — or deliberate pixel grid, or subtle optical grain only
Clean is the texture here.

**Color Archetypes**
- Black + white (primary)
- Deep blue + electric blue + white
- Black + single neon (lime, cyan, electric orange)

**Key References**
Apple SF type system, Bauhaus typography experiments,
Emigre magazine era digital modernism, Wim Crouwel grid work,
IBM Plex type system, Google identity 2015 onward

**Prompt Activation Vocabulary**
`modern geometric sans-serif wordmark, Swiss grid influence` /
`Bauhaus-inspired typography, pure geometric construction` /
`tech brand identity mark, geometric sans with mathematical precision` /
`clean monospace display type, digital modernism aesthetic` /
`minimalist geometric logo type, negative space as design element`

## 20. DARK AMERICANA / AMERICANA GOTHIC

**Aesthetic DNA**
The darker cousin of Heritage Americana. Where whiskey, tattoo culture,
prison chapel folk art, Johnny Cash, and rural gothic meet. Revelator Coffee
meets Nick Cave meets a roadside diner in Mississippi that hasn't changed since 1958.

**Letterform Character**
- Blackletter influence but Americanized — heavy strokes, reduced decoration
- Gothic condensed with visible pen angle
- Occasional brush-painted fat caps
- Flourish used sparingly, with weight and gravitas
- Never whimsical — always earned

**Texture Defaults**
Barrel char, burnt wood grain, inked skin (tattoo parlor flash aesthetic),
old bar menu chalkboard, hand-lettered diner counter sign

**Color Archetypes**
- Black + red + cream
- Black + gold + aged white
- Single color black — burned in

**Key References**
Johnny Cash concert poster typography, Old Crow bourbon label (pre-update),
Jack Daniel's label language, vintage tattoo flash card lettering,
David Goines poster work (as contrast point), Southern Comfort vintage labels,
Nick Cave and the Bad Seeds tour poster work

**Prompt Activation Vocabulary**
`dark americana gothic lettering` / `whiskey label blackletter, Americana Gothic` /
`tattoo flash card typographic aesthetic` / `Southern gothic poster lettering` /
`diner hand-lettered sign aesthetic, heavy gothic brush caps` /
`Americana noir typography, dark heritage label`
