About this piece

The Juniper came out of a long period of looking at what was wrong with structured bags. Too boxy, too boxy, always too boxy. The triangular flap was the answer: a geometric signal that says this bag has a point of view. It angles to a peak at the center, secured by a single turn-lock, and the whole front becomes a composition instead of a panel.

The silhouette is deliberately narrow front-to-back. This is a European profile, designed to move with the body rather than project away from it. The width is enough for a daily carry — wallet, keys, phone, a folded notebook — but the bag stays close. It behaves well in a restaurant, on a train, at an opening.

The Details

  • Italian pebble grain leather body — milled for softness with visible texture that softens further with wear
  • Optional smooth grain leather trim at flap edge and handle join
  • Full pigskin lining with leather interfacing for structure and shape retention
  • Bonded nylon thread — stronger than waxed linen, flatter seam profile
  • Antique brass or nickel turn-lock and hardware throughout
  • Short handle: structured and shaped for arm carry
  • Crossbody strap: adjustable with solid hardware, same finish as bag
  • Interior: one flat pocket, one zip pocket, card slots
  • Edges hand-painted and burnished
  • Made one at a time by a single maker
